Chapter 1

Getting Started

New to photography? Have a nice DSLR camera (or the latest smartphone) and want to take advantage of it? Here are a few tips and tricks to help you get started into the wonderful world of photography.

 

 

 

 

Tip 1 : Read the Manual

(1) Camera manual

Your camera has plenty of settings that allow you to adjust a myriad of options (aperture, ISO, shutter speed, white balance, etc..). Familiarize yourself with the camera settings now instead of experimenting on the fly (which might make you miss taking photos!).

Note: Depending on your smartphone, there might only be a few settings that you could manipulate. Downloading an app like VSCO will allow you to access more of your smartphone's internal settings.

 

Tip 2 : Set the Highest Resolution Possible

(2) Resolution

High-res photos have more detail - which makes the image more vivid and precise. Also, if you need to you can crop it later and still have a good resolution picture that you can use.

Note: High-res photos = bigger file sizes. You'll need a bigger memory card (or allot bigger space for photos on your smartphone) to accommodate the increased file size.
